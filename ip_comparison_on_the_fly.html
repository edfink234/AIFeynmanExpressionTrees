Horizontal
\[ x = x_p(t) + l \sin (\theta) = A \cos(\omega t) + l \sin (\theta), \] \[ y = l \cos (\theta) \] The velocity components of the pendulum bob are: \[ \dot{x} = -A \omega \sin(\omega t) + l \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta), \] \[ \dot{y} = -l \dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \] The total kinetic energy \( T \) is therefore: \[ T = \frac{1}{2} m (\dot{x}^2 + \dot{y}^2) \] Expanding this expression, we get: \[ T = \frac{1}{2} m \left[ \left(-A \omega \sin(\omega t) + l \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta)\right)^2 + \left(l \dot{\theta} \sin (\theta)\right)^2 \right] \] Expanding further and simplifying, we obtain: \[ T = \frac{1}{2} m \left( A^2 \omega^2 \sin^2(\omega t) + l^2 \dot{\theta}^2 - 2 A l \omega \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) \right) \] The potential energy \( V \) is due to gravity and is given by: \[ V = -m g y = -m g l \cos (\theta) \] The Lagrangian \( L \) is given by: \[ L = T - V = \frac{1}{2} m \left( A^2 \omega^2 \sin^2(\omega t) + l^2 \dot{\theta}^2 - 2 A l \omega \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) \right) + m g l \cos (\theta) \] Using the Euler-Lagrange equation: \[ \frac{d}{dt} \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta}} \right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial \theta} = 0, \] \[ \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta}} = m l^2 \dot{\theta} - m A l \omega \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t). \] \[ \frac{d}{dt} \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta}} \right) = m l^2 \ddot{\theta} - m A l \omega \left( -\dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) \] \[ \frac{\partial L}{\partial \theta} = m l \left( A \omega \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \sin (\theta) - g \sin (\theta) \right) \] \[ m l^2 \ddot{\theta} - m A l \omega \left( -\dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) - m l \left( A \omega \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \sin (\theta) - g \sin (\theta) \right) = 0 \] \[ l \ddot{\theta} - A \omega \left( -\dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) - \left( A \omega \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \sin (\theta) - g \sin (\theta) \right) = 0 \] \[ l \ddot{\theta} - A \omega \left( -\dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) + \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \sin (\theta) \right) + g \sin (\theta) = 0 \] \[ \ddot{\theta} - \frac{ A \omega}{l} \left( -\dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) + \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \sin (\theta) \right) + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = 0 \] \[ \ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = \frac{ A \omega}{l} \left( -\dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) + \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \sin (\theta) \right) \] \[ \ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = \frac{ A \omega}{l} \left( \cos (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) \] \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = \frac{ A \omega^2}{l} \left( \cos (\theta) \cos(\omega t) \right)} \label{eq:horizontal_original_no_damping} \tag{1} \end{equation} Expanding about \(\theta = \pi\) yields to first order \(\sin(\theta) \sim 0 + \cos(\pi) (\theta-\pi) = \pi - \theta\), \(\cos(\theta) \sim -1\), yielding \[ \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l}(\pi - \theta) = -\frac{ A \omega^2}{l}\cos(\omega t)} \] with solution \[\theta{\left(t \right)} = \frac{A \omega^{2} \cos{\left(\omega t \right)}}{g + l \omega^{2}} + C_{1} e^{- \frac{\sqrt{g} t}{\sqrt{l}}} + C_{2} e^{\frac{\sqrt{g} t}{\sqrt{l}}} + \pi\] To second order, \(\sin(\theta) \sim (\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\), \(\cos(\theta) \sim -1 + \dfrac{(\theta-\pi)^2}{2}\), yielding \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l}\left((\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\right) = \frac{ A \omega^2}{l}\left(-1 + \dfrac{(\theta-\pi)^2}{2}\right)\cos(\omega t)} \label{eq:hoizontal_second_order_no_damping} \tag{2} \end{equation} Considering linear damping simply modifies equation \ref{eq:horizontal_original_no_damping} to be: \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = \frac{ A \omega^2}{l} \left( \cos (\theta) \cos(\omega t) \right) -\lambda \dot{\theta}}\label{eq:horizontal_original_linear_damping}\tag{3} \end{equation} and equation \ref{eq:hoizontal_second_order_no_damping} to be: \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l}\left((\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\right) = \frac{ A \omega^2}{l}\left(-1 + \dfrac{(\theta-\pi)^2}{2}\right)\cos(\omega t) -\lambda \dot{\theta}}\label{eq:hoizontal_second_order_linear_damping}\tag{4} \end{equation}
Vertical
\[ x = l \sin (\theta), \] \[ y = y_p(t) + l \cos (\theta) = A \cos(\omega t) + l \cos (\theta) \] The velocity components of the pendulum bob are: \[ \dot{x} = l \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta), \] \[ \dot{y} = -A \omega \sin(\omega t) - l \dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \] The total kinetic energy \( T \) is therefore: \[ T = \frac{1}{2} m (\dot{x}^2 + \dot{y}^2) \] Expanding this expression, we get: \[ T = \frac{1}{2} m \left[ \left(l \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta)\right)^2 + \left(-A \omega \sin(\omega t) - l \dot{\theta} \sin (\theta)\right)^2 \right] \] Expanding further and simplifying, we obtain: \[ T = \frac{1}{2} m \left( A^2 \omega^2 \sin^2(\omega t) + l^2 \dot{\theta}^2 + 2 A l \omega \dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) \right) \] The potential energy \( V \) is due to gravity and is given by: \[ V = -m g y = -m g \left(A \cos (\omega t) + l \cos (\theta)\right) \] The Lagrangian \( L \) is given by: \[ L = T - V = \frac{1}{2} m \left( A^2 \omega^2 \sin^2(\omega t) + l^2 \dot{\theta}^2 + 2 A l \omega \dot{\theta} \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t) \right) + m g \left(A \cos (\omega t) + l \cos (\theta)\right) \] Using the Euler-Lagrange equation: \[ \frac{d}{dt} \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta}} \right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial \theta} = 0, \] \[ \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta}} = m l^2 \dot{\theta} + m A l \omega \sin (\theta) \sin(\omega t). \] \[ \frac{d}{dt} \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta}} \right) = m l^2 \ddot{\theta} + m A l \omega \left( \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \sin (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) \] \[ \frac{\partial L}{\partial \theta} = m l \left( A \omega \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \cos (\theta) - g \sin (\theta) \right) \] \[ m l^2 \ddot{\theta} + m A l \omega \left( \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \sin (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) - m l \left( A \omega \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \cos (\theta) - g \sin (\theta) \right) = 0 \] \[ l \ddot{\theta} + A \omega \left( \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \sin (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) - \left( A \omega \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \cos (\theta) - g \sin (\theta) \right) = 0 \] \[ l \ddot{\theta} + A \omega \left( \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \sin (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) - \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \cos (\theta) \right) + g \sin (\theta) = 0 \] \[ \ddot{\theta} + \frac{A \omega}{l} \left( \dot{\theta} \cos (\theta) \sin(\omega t) + \sin (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) - \dot{\theta} \sin(\omega t) \cos (\theta) \right) + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = 0 \] \[ \ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = -\frac{A \omega}{l} \left( \sin (\theta) \omega \cos(\omega t) \right) \] \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = -\frac{A \omega^2}{l} \left( \sin (\theta) \cos(\omega t) \right)} \label{eq:vertical_original_no_damping} \tag{5} \end{equation} Expanding about \(\theta = \pi\) yields to first order \(\sin(\theta) \sim 0 + \cos(\pi) (\theta-\pi) = \pi - \theta\), \(\cos(\theta) \sim -1\), yielding: \[ \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} (\pi - \theta) = -\frac{A \omega^2}{l} \left( (\pi - \theta) \cos(\omega t) \right)} \] To second order, \(\sin(\theta) \sim (\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\), \(\cos(\theta) \sim -1 + \dfrac{(\theta-\pi)^2}{2}\), yielding \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \left((\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\right) = -\frac{A \omega^2}{l} \left( \left((\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\right) \cos(\omega t) \right)} \label{eq:vertical_second_order_no_damping} \tag{6} \end{equation} Considering linear damping simply modifies equation \ref{eq:vertical_original_no_damping} to be: \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \sin (\theta) = -\frac{A \omega^2}{l} \left( \sin (\theta) \cos(\omega t) \right) -\lambda \dot{\theta}}\label{eq:vertical_original_linear_damping} \tag{7} \end{equation} and equation \ref{eq:vertical_second_order_no_damping} to be: \begin{equation} \boxed{\ddot{\theta} + \frac{g}{l} \left((\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\right) = -\frac{A \omega^2}{l} \left( \left((\pi - \theta) + \dfrac{(\theta - \pi)^3}{6}\right) \cos(\omega t) \right) -\lambda \dot{\theta}}\label{eq:vertical_second_order_linear_damping} \tag{8} \end{equation}
